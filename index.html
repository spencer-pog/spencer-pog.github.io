about me

I am currently a doctoral student in the Department of Linguistics at the Ohio State University.

My primary research focus is theoretical morphology, especially paradigm structure and morphological complexity. I am also interested in examining broader morphosyntactic phenomena in Spanish as a whole and in individual dialects. I study these topics using quantitative and corpus methods. My recent master's thesis, Dialectal Variation and Morphological Complexity: A Study of Spanish Verbal Systems, examined and compared the inflectional complexity of Spanish dialects using multiple complexity measures.

I received my MA in Linguistics from San Diego State University in 2025, and my BA in Linguistics, with a secondary major in Spanish Studies, from Brigham Young University in 2022.
